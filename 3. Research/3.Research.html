  • 3d Metal Catalysis

The Bera Group explores the fundamental organometallic chemistry required to rationally design and synthesize catalysts for the activation, functionalization and conversion of challenging substrates to useful chemicals using transition-metals preferably earth-abundant 3d metals. Inspired by the synergic cooperation between two or more chemical functionalities in natural metallo-enzymes to execute difficult chemical processes, we have developed catalysts featuring metal-metal or metal-ligand cooperation. The ongoing studies focus on fabricating catalysts bearing proton-responsive functionalities on the ligand scaffold for a wide variety of challenging hydrogenation and dehydrogenation reactions. Protic moities act as a source or sink of a proton in the proton/hydride transfer reactions, thereby enabling easily accessible, alternate mechanistic pathway to that of classical catalysts devoid of such functionalities.

 

Ligand Design

Intelligent ligand design is the backbone of our research. In particular, we have been fascinated by the unique stereo-electronic properties of the naphthyridine-functionalized annellated N-heterocyclic carbenes (NHCs) and mesoionic carbenes (MICs). Several transition metal compounds bearing these newly designed ligands have been shown to be effective catalysts for diverse oxidative and reductive transformations.

Small Molecule Activation

Small molecule activation is a real challenge in the chemistry due to less dipole moment and high activation energy barrier. We could activate hydrogen, carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, water by transition metal complexes in different reactions. We have designed catalysts featuring pendant basic functional groups (pyridyl- or 1,8-naphthyridyl), which engage in secondary interactions with water, thus assisting its nucleophilic attack to a metal-bound substrate. Using this design principle we demonstrated oxygenation of the Ir-bound cyclooctadiene and developed effective Ru(II), Rh(I) and Au(I) catalysts for the conversion of alcohols to carboxylic acid, organonitriles to amides and the alkynes to amides, respectively.

Catalysis and Mechanism Study

Catalysis reactions involving addition/elimination of molecules to organic functionalities is our primary focus. We aim to develop novel and highly efficient catalysts. Importantly, we keenly seek to garner detailed mechanictic understanding of the catalytic transformations using experimental and computational methods. Ultimately, a fundamental mechanistic understanding drives the development of novel catalytic reactions.

Bimetallic Synergy

The prospect of bimetallic synergy in organometallic catalysis has driven our interest in metal-metal bonded compounds. We have developed several dicopper, diruthenium, dirhodium and dipalladium compounds and demonstrated their catalytic applications. High catalytic activities have been obtained which are credited to metal-metal cooperation.

Green Chemistry

Modern-day demands easy products formation under environmentally benign condition. Atom economy and high yield is another requirement. Hydrogen is a source of cleaner energy. We generate hydrogen efficiently in different reaction to address the production of the sustainable energy problem. Water as a solvent in organometallic reactions offers valuable prospect in terms of its economic, safety, and environmental considerations. ‘On-water’ reaction provides ketone from alkyne. Role of the fluorinated anion is evaluated.
